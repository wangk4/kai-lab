Welcome

We are a research group in the School of Public Health at Indiana University Bloomington, studying how environmental exposures affect human health through molecular and epigenetic pathways. Our work focuses on environmental epigenetics, piRNAs, and integrative exposomics using computational and experimental tools.

The lab is currently supported by a NIEHS K99/R00 Award.

Research

  • Machine Learning for Exposomics: Predicting molecular targets and tissues of environmental chemical exposures.
  • Environmental Epigenetics: Impact of toxicants on DNA methylation and chromatin.
  • piRNA Biology: Roles of piRNAs beyond the germline in environmental response.
  • Multi-omics Integration: Combining genomics, transcriptomics, and exposure data.
